The interplay between predation and competition

Answers to the reading theory exercise

Make sure you have read your handout (or download the handout here) for the summary of this paper.

Question 1: Do you buy the insight that resource competition and predation may play similar roles in focal species coexistence?

Conceptually, it is a much older idea that predators can affect the competitive outcomes of focal species similar to resource competitions, a phenomenon termed “apparent competition”. However, whether their contributions are quantitatively comparable depends on how the focal species interact with the predators and resources.

Question 2: Notice the assumptions of logistic growth by both resources and predators. The resources have an intrinsic rate of increase and a carrying capacity. Is this a good characterization of a biotic or abiotic resource?

Biotic resources since they follow logistic growths.

Question 3: If the resources are abiotic, would they still play a similar role as predators in determining coexistence? Are there any simplifications this model makes that might lead predators and resources to have qualitatively different effects on focal species coexistence?

Our additional analyses suggest that the niche overlap remains affected by both the predators and the abiotic resources, but the exact mathematical relationship is now different. However, niche overlap is only one factor that affect coexistence; the other factor (difference in species’ average competitive abilities; see the original paper for details) between the two focal species now seems to solely depend on their predators rather than both. Some examples of other model simplifications include: finite population sizes (noise in small populations), functional responses for the predation and resource consumption, no adaptive behaviors (e.g. prey switching) or coevolution, but their specific effect on model results will need further theoretical assessment.
