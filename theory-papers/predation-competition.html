The interplay between predation and competition

Answers to the reading theory exercise

Make sure you have read your handout (or download the handout here) for the summary of this paper.

Question 1: Are the resources here abiotic or biotic?

Biotic resources since they follow logistic growths.

Question 2: If the resources are abiotic, would they still play a similar role as predators in determining coexistence?

With further theoretical assessment, the fitness difference between the two focal species now solely depends on their predators. On the other hand, the niche overlap remains affected by both the predators and the abiotic resources, but the exact mathematical expression is now different.

Question 3: What if instead of two predators, the focal species share two mutualists or two microbiomes (which can have either positive or negative effects on the focal species)?

Inspired by this study, Kandlikar et al. (2019) derived the niche overlap and fitness differences in a system where two plant species compete for resources while also interact with soil microbes. Similarly, they found plant-plant interactions was more strongly influenced by soil microbes under high resource level but by resource competition under low resource level.
