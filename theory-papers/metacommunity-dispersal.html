Answers to the reading theory exercise

Make sure you have read your handout (or download the handout here) for the summary of this paper.

Question 1: How is habitat preference characterized in their model when there are as many habitat types as patches? Are there any generalists?

Each species is the superior competitor in one habitat type, so that each species has its own “niche” habitat. The authors have assumed there are no generalists (unless there the habitat heterogeneity was set at 1, i.e. all patches are identical).

Question 2: A commonly theorized trade-off in spatially organized communities is the competition-colonization trade-off, where species can be stronger competitors and can displace other species but at the cost of dispersal ability, and vice versa. Would you expect to observe a competition-colonization trade-off across species in this model? Why or why not?

This model would not have and could not show such a trade-off, because the per-capita dispersal rates are constant across all species.

Question 3: In your study system, is having equal dispersal rates across species a reasonable assumption? If not, does that lack of realism change the value of the model for you?
