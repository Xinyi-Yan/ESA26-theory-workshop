Impact of intraguild predation and stage structure on simple communities along a productivity gradient

Answers to the reading theory exercise

Make sure you have read your handout (or download the handout here) for the summary of this paper.

Question 1: The predator in the model is called an “intraguild” predator. What makes the predation in this model “intraguild” predation?

The predator consumes the prey, but it also competes with the prey. Because both the predator and prey share the same resource, they are in the same guild. The combination of predation and competition within a guild leads to the term “intraguild predation”.

Question 2: According to the model, what happens at low, intermediate, and high productivity?

At low productivity, the predator is not present and only the prey persists. At intermediate productivity, the predator and the prey coexist. At high productivity, the prey is excluded by the predator.

Question 3: The model assumes that the consumer is the better competitor for the shared resource. Why might this assumption matter for the result?

If both species were equally matched competitors, then the predator would be at an advantage because the predator would have an additional food source (the intraguild prey). So the prey must have some compensating advantage to persist, which in this model is being the superior competitor for the shared resource. Without that advantage, the predator would likely outcompete the consumer, and there would be no coexistence.

Question 4: What assumption in this model would be most likely to fail in your study system?

Depends on the study system, but some possibilities include:

  • There is only one shared resource.
  • The intraguild prey is always the superior competitor.
  • The populations are well-mixed.
  • There are no differences among individuals of the same species.
  • The resource is always present and does not fluctuate through time.
  • Other assumptions that are implicit in the description provided or the accompanying diagram.
